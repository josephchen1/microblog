1970-1972 Êó•Êú¨, Êù±‰∫¨ Tokyo, Japan

Located in the heart of Tokyo, Kurokawa Kisho’s Nakagin Capsule Tower housed 140 self-contained units. The small apartments were designed for short-term stays by people who worked in the urban centre while living in the suburbs, reflecting new trends in how people moved throughout the city. The capsules were marketed as additional office space, secondary residences, or simple sites to recharge near the workplace. The tower was one of the few buildings realised as part of the 1960s Japanese architectural movement known as Metabolism. The name of the movement was a biological metaphor for how architecture could be designed with the ability to evolve, accommodating growth and changing societal needs. Attached to a concrete core service tower, the Nakagin capsules could, in theory, be replaced, which would extend the overall lifespan of the building. However, the building fell into disrepair in the early 2000s.

Despite numerous attempts to save it, it was demolished in 2022. Some capsules were carefully restored and collected by museums around the world, such as Unit A806 at M+.

Region No. 43701-No. 43900 (1998) LED mounted on aluminium, circuitry

Miyajima Tatsuo born 1957, Japan works Ibaraki

Two hundred LED counters flash numbers between 1 and 99 in red and green in endless loops. After reaching 99, the counter returns to 1. Numbers with the digit 0, a symbol of finality and emptiness for the artist, are omitted. The work may evoke affectless computer code but was in fact inspired by Miyajima’s experiences in the hospitals he frequented as a chronically ill child. The continual flashing and counting of numbers illustrate the Buddhist concept of samsara, the ceaseless cycle of reincarnation.

ok so after an hour dooming and researching outside because I can’t sell my car without passing smog. anyways I went to a cafe to collect my thoughts bc the smog check place said it’d be $150 for diagnosis.

so tires. I called DC Tires bc a AAA mechanic brought gabi and mimi there once to get a new tire and apparently a really good experience + good price. they were indeed nice and said they got nothing that old in stock, that being 24 years old model. fair. they recommended me to buy a tire online and they can switch it for me. as for oil change, they didn’t do it but referred me to an oil change shop, which would top off my other fluids, too. not sure what other fluids there are tbh. i know there are other fluids, but how good is that deal persay.
